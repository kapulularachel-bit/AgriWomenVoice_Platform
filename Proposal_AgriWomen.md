# Project Proposal: AgriWomen Voice Platform

## Abstract

AgriWomen Voice Platform is a voice-first digital solution to empower female smallholder farmers in Malawi by giving them access to loans, land registration, market prices, weather forecasts, education materials, and direct communication with buyers, NGOs and extension officers. The platform emphasizes low-bandwidth, low-literacy access via USSD and IVR, with a progressive web interface for smartphone users and administrators. The platform will bridge information gaps, improve market access, and increase women's control over produce and income.

## 1. Introduction and Background

Malawi's economy relies heavily on agriculture. Women comprise a large proportion of smallholder and subsistence farmers but face systematic barriers to technology, market access and financial services. The platform builds on existing interventions (e.g., Ulangizi, KOICA/UN Women greenhouses) to provide a voice-first, inclusive digital channel that is adapted to rural contexts.

## 2. Problem Statement

Female farmers in rural Malawi face limited access to modern agricultural technology, market information, and finance. Low digital literacy and device constraints mean smartphone-first solutions exclude many users. Without reliable weather and price information, farmers get lower incomes and remain vulnerable to climate shocks.

## 3. Objectives

### General Objective
To develop a platform that enables female farmers to access digital agricultural services and information, increasing productivity and income.

### Specific Objectives
- Provide online loan access and tracking.
- Provide land registration support and integration points.
- Deliver localized weather forecasting and alerts.
- Publish real-time market price updates.
- Enable direct buyer–farmer connections.
- Provide basic agricultural education via voice and SMS.
- Connect farmers with NGOs and extension officers for support.

## 4. Significance of the Study

- Economic: Increased market access, higher negotiated prices, and better yield decisions.
- Social: Empowerment of women with better financial control and reduced reliance on intermediaries.
- Operational: Streamlined communication between farmers, NGOs and extension services.

## 5. Methodology

Agile methodology will guide development: iterative sprints with stakeholder feedback (farmers, extension officers, NGOs). Key stages: requirements gathering (field interviews), design (IVR scripts, wireframes), development (MVP: registration, weather, market, listings), pilot testing (200–500 farmers), and rollout.

### Fieldwork and User Research
- Conduct interviews and focus groups in Kasungu, Dowa, and Dedza with representative sample groups.
- Run usability tests for IVR flows and SMS content.

## 6. Tools & Technologies
